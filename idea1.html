Selling Beverages Near College Campus

1. Target Specificity

Broad targeting of "college students" lacks precision. Segment must be narrowed by time, behavior, or urgency.

2. Incentive Structure

Primary incentive is convenience and immediate craving satisfaction. Low switching cost.

3. Revenue Logic

High volume, low margin model. Dependent on foot traffic density.

4. Substitution Risk

Extremely high. Nearby shops, vending machines, homemade alternatives.

5. Defensibility

Weak unless location control or exclusive supply advantage exists.

6. Long-Term Viability

Stable if location is secured, fragile if competition increases.
